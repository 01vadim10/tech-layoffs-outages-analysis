## Twitter/X Data

### Layoffs (75% Workforce Reduction)

**Primary Sources:**
- Elon Musk told investors he planned to cut 75% of Twitter's workforce, reducing it from 7,500 to around 2,000 employees
- Twitter's trust and safety head confirmed overall headcount was cut by roughly 50%
- By April 2023, Musk had reduced the workforce from around 8,000 employees to approximately 1,500

**Source URLs:**
- Washington Post: "Musk's plan to cut 75% of Twitter staff"
- The Verge: "Twitter layoffs tracker"
- TechCrunch: "Twitter workforce reductions 2022-2023"

### Twitter/X Outages

**July 1, 2023**: Thousands of Twitter users reported "rate limit exceeded" errors
- Source: DownDetector, multiple tech news outlets

**July 12, 2023**: Over 7,100 user complaints about Twitter glitches and outages
- Source: DownDetector incident reports

**December 21, 2023**: Twitter/X went down globally for nearly an hour
- Source: Reuters, BBC Technology

**March 2025**: "Massive cyber attack" caused sporadic outages according to Musk
- Source: Elon Musk's official statements, tech news coverage

## Meta (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp) Data

**November 2022**: Meta laid off 11,000 employees (13% of workforce)
- Source: Meta official announcement, Mark Zuckerberg memo

**March 2023**: Meta announced an additional 10,000 job cuts
- Source: Meta SEC filings, company press releases

**Total**: Approximately 21,000 people lost their jobs at Meta between November 2022 and March 2023

**Source URLs:**
- Meta Investor Relations: Official announcements

### Meta Service Outages

**March 5, 2024**: Major Facebook and Instagram outage

- Duration: Approximately 2 hours
- Source: DownDetector, Meta engineering blog, major news outlets

**December 11, 2024**: Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp disrupted for thousands of users
- Source: DownDetector, user reports, company statements

## Methodology Notes

### Data Collection
- Layoff numbers sourced from official company announcements and SEC filings
- Outage data collected from DownDetector reports and official company status pages
- Cross-referenced with major tech news outlets (The Verge, TechCrunch, Reuters, Bloomberg)

### Incident Classification
An "incident" or "major outage" is defined as:
- Service disruption affecting 10,000+ users
- Duration of 30+ minutes
- Reported on DownDetector with 1,000+ user reports
- Officially acknowledged by company or widely covered in tech press

### Limitations
- Not all outages are publicly reported
- Small incidents may not be captured
- DownDetector data reflects reported issues, not total affected users
- Some incidents may have multiple causes beyond staffing
